# Benln Cold Email Outreach

Write cold emails that are 2-3 sentences and read like a real text from a thoughtful human. Every email must include a punchy subject line, meaningful personalization, and one clear ask.

Avoid signatures, fluff, and vague requests such as "pick your brain" or "hop on a quick call sometime." Keep the writing specific, direct, and natural enough that it does not look like AI was use[...]

## Workflow

When the user provides prospect details, identify:

- who the recipient is
- what is genuinely specific about them or their company
- what value proposition is most relevant to them
- what single ask should close the email

Then write:

1. One subject line.
2. One email body in 2-3 sentences.

## Writing Rules

- Lead with a concrete, relevant personalization point.
- Keep the message tight; remove filler, hedging, and generic compliments.
- Make the ask explicit and easy to answer.
- Use plainspoken language that feels like a real operator wrote it.
- Do not add a signature block unless the user explicitly asks for one.
- Do not use vague CTA language such as "pick your brain," "connect," or "let me know if interested."
- Do not mention AI, templates, or automation.

## Output Format

Use this structure:

**Subject:** <subject line>

<2-3 sentence email body>
